ARO is a democratic organisation of over 6,000 reclaimers. We fight for recognition, fair pay, and justice — because the people who recycle South Africa's waste deserve to be treated with dignity.

Fighting for Change

Campaigns

ARO is a campaigning organisation. Our campaigns build pressure on government and industry, mobilise our members, and win recognition and rights for reclaimers.

Putting Vision Into Practice

Programmes

ARO's programmes demonstrate concretely that a reclaimer-inclusive model of waste management works better for everyone — for reclaimers, residents, municipalities, and the environment.

Shaping National Policy

Policy Engagement

Real change requires changes in law and policy. ARO has been a consistent and effective voice in South Africa's national policy debates, pushing for frameworks that formally recognise and pay reclaimers.

Global Solidarity

International Work

The struggle for reclaimer rights is not only a South African struggle. ARO has built genuine relationships with reclaimer organisations and international policy processes across the world.

The Movement's Heart

The Reclaimers

Close to 90,000 South Africans work as informal reclaimers, doing close to 90% of all recycling in this country. ARO members are these workers — skilled, organised, and fighting for their rights.

Get Involved

Whether you are a resident, a business, a researcher, or a reclaimer — there is a role for you in this struggle.

The most important thing a resident can do is simple: when you put out your recyclables, put them out in a way that reclaimers can access. Contact ARO to join the Recycling with Reclaimers programme in your area.

For businesses and corporates: buy from reclaimers, partner with ARO, and ensure your recycling contracts support the people doing the work.

For reclaimers: join ARO, register on SAWPRS, and organise together for recognition and fair pay.
